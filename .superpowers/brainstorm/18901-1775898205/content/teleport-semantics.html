How should "teleport" work?

Pick the semantic that feels right — everything downstream (solver, generator, 25 level files) depends on this choice. Each example shows a swipe right on a 5×5 board.

A

Post-swipe pair portals (recommended)

Portals come in linked pairs (A↔B) placed on cells. The swipe resolves normally. After tiles settle, any tile sitting on portal A jumps to portal B (and vice-versa), but only if the destination cell is empty. No mid-slide magic.

B

Mid-slide pair portals

Portals are traversed during the slide: a tile entering portal A is instantly relocated to portal B and keeps sliding in the same direction from there. Multiple tiles can pass through in one swipe.

Solver cost: significant — slide physics now jump between rows/cols. Multi-portal chains need care. Feels like: exciting, action-y, but unpredictable for designers.

C

Edge wrap (Pac-Man)

No portals at all — selected rows or columns wrap around. A tile sliding off the right edge of a wrap row reappears on the left edge of the same row and keeps sliding.

Solver cost: tiny — just a line modifier. Feels like: clean and puzzly, but less visually striking than portals.

D

Swap portals

Paired cells A/B. When a tile lands on A (after slide), whatever is at B swaps places with it — even if B holds a tile. Effectively "send this tile where the other tile was."

Solver cost: moderate. Feels like: a transposition trick — interesting but conceptually harder to teach.
