Teleport exit direction — three deterministic options

You asked about random — here's why it doesn't work, and three options that keep the puzzle solvable.

Why not random? 2048-style levels are puzzles — they need one optimal solution the solver can find, so we can score stars, set par moves, and prove the level is fair. A random exit makes the same swipe lead to four different boards, so there is no "optimal". The player can't plan, the solver can't solve, and difficulty tuning falls apart.
B1

Portal B has a fixed exit direction (my recommendation)

Each portal-B cell has an arrow the level designer sets (↑/↓/←/→). When a tile lands on portal A, it jumps to B and then slides in the direction the arrow points, until it hits something. Designer-controlled, deterministic, and visually clear.

Solver cost: small. Adds a deterministic post-slide resolution step per portal. Feels like: each portal is a little launcher the designer aims.

B2

Tile continues in the original swipe direction

If you swiped right and a tile lands on portal A, it pops out at B and keeps sliding right from there. The direction just inherits your swipe. Simple, no portal-metadata needed.

Solver cost: small. Feels like: predictable — portals are pure "shortcuts" in the same direction. Fewer design levers.

B3

Player chooses the exit direction

When a tile hits portal A, the game pauses and asks the player "which way does it go?" (↑/↓/←/→). Adds agency, but interrupts the tactile swipe feel.

Solver cost: the BFS branches by 4× at every teleport — still tractable but slower to generate levels. Feels like: a strategy pause every time you teleport.

If you like the "it keeps moving" vibe, B1 gives the richest design space — a portal with a ↑ aims tiles up, a portal with ← shoots them left, and you can build puzzles around those launchers.
